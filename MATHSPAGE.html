Who is Brahmagupta?

Brahmagupta was an Indian mathematician and astronomer who lived in the 7th century CE.

He was born in Bhinmal, in present day Rajasthan. Brahmagupta was best known for his

discovery of Arithmetic and Algebra, Geometry, and the natural number '0'. One of the

main findings was of '0'.



In ancient times, it was not know to denote nothing, as something, so at that time, '0'

was not considered as a number. But it was not until 628 AD, that '0' became an important

part of the number system. Brahmagupta, an Indian mathematician and astronomer, took the idea

of nothingness, and symbolized it as an oval. althoug, Brahmaguta gave us the rules to use zero

in calculations defining its very first use in calculations. But he never introduced zero for its

second purpose.



Aryabhata's contribution on Brahmagupta's idea.

Everything that we used '0' for was not only because

of Brahmagupta's discovery. It was another mathematician

Aryabhata, who used '0' as a placeholder. Soon, this

revolutionary knowledge spread around the world. And,

mathematicians in other places started using '0' in their calculations.
